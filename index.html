Visit Cape May


Cape May is a seaside town located on the southernmost tip of New Jersey. It is a vibrent community that is defined by its quaint Victorian architecture, amazing food, and breathtaking ocean views. Cape May was established in 1611 as a whaling town which is evidenced by the historic architexture and many public museums.

This is an image of a cape may beach. It is very pretty during both the summer and winter. Pictured on the left are some of the many historic Victorian homes that cape may has to offer. These houses are protected by the Cape May Historical Sociaty. The beach has lifeguards during the summer and dedicated surfing zones. On clear days its even possible to see across the water to Delaware!

This is an image of Cape May at night! Since there are no lights on the beach Cape May is the perfect place for stargazing. There is not much light pollution and there are no tall buildings to obsruct the view!

There are many native species in Cape May such as horseshoe crabs, bald eagles, dolphins and more!

The winter season in this seaside town is extremely festive too! Many of the towns historic landmarks are decorated and the city hosts a Christmas Parade.

This video is of my family and participating in the Cape May Turkey Trot. It is an annual 5k run on Thanksgiving and an extremely vibrent event! The run takes place next to a beautiful beach. If you are adventurous you could even take a swim in the frigid November ocean!
